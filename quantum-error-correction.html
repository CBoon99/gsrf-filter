Quantum error correction

Belief propagation (BP) is a promising route to fast decoders at scale — but BP can stall on surface-code structures. Zero Overshoot (GSRF) applies an external preprocessing layer to the reliability landscape so BP can converge in fewer iterations, without altering the BP update rules themselves.

This page summarises validated numerical workbench results (not circuit-level quantum hardware claims). Algorithms match the standalone quantum decoder demo and the broader test corpus described on the homepage.

Summary

Validated claims (surface code, sampled errors)
  • At distance d=9 (81 qubits), GSRF + BP at 4 iterations outperforms unassisted BP at 10: about 66.7% vs 48.8% logical success in the cited bench configuration.
  • About 2.5× fewer never-converge failures in the same setting; advantage traced from smaller d through d=13 in the numerical study.
  • MWPM scales superlinearly; GSRF+BP retains linear-time decoding structure suitable for parallel implementation.
